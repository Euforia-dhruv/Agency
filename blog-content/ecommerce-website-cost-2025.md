Building an online store is one of the best investments you can make for your business. But the cost varies wildly depending on your needs.

This guide breaks down exactly what you'll pay for an e-commerce website in 2025.

## E-Commerce Pricing Tiers

### Basic Store ($800–$2,000)

Best for: New businesses testing e-commerce

- Up to 50 products
- Standard checkout flow
- 2-3 payment gateways
- Basic product pages
- Mobile responsive

### Standard Store ($2,000–$5,000)

Best for: Growing brands

- Up to 500 products
- Advanced filtering and search
- Customer accounts and wishlists
- Inventory management
- Order tracking
- Email integration

### Enterprise Store ($5,000–$15,000+)

Best for: Established brands scaling up

- Unlimited products
- Multi-warehouse inventory
- Custom product configurators
- Subscription/recurring billing
- Multi-language and multi-currency
- Advanced analytics and reporting

## Key Cost Factors

### Product Catalog Size

More products = more development time for data entry, images, and SEO optimization.

### Payment Gateways

- Razorpay: 2% per transaction
- Stripe: 2.9% + $0.30
- PhonePe/GPay: 1-2%
- Multiple gateways increase dev cost

### Integrations

Each integration adds to the cost:

- Accounting software (Zoho, QuickBooks)
- Shipping (Shiprocket, Delhivery)
- Email marketing (Mailchimp, Klaviyo)
- ERP systems
