## Gym Website Pricing Tiers

### Basic Website ($600–$1,200)

Best for: New gyms, personal trainers, small studios

- 5 pages (Home, About, Classes, Pricing, Contact)
- Mobile-responsive design
- Contact form & Google Maps
- Basic SEO optimization
- Content management system
- 2 rounds of revisions

### Standard Website ($1,200–$2,500)

Best for: Established gyms, CrossFit boxes, yoga studios

Everything in Basic, plus:

- Class schedule with live availability
- Trainer profiles and certifications
- Online booking system
- Member registration forms
- Photo gallery and virtual tour
- Blog setup for SEO
- Google Analytics integration

### Premium Website ($2,500–$5,000+)

Best for: Large fitness chains, multi-location gyms

Everything in Standard, plus:

- Online payment integration (monthly memberships, class passes)
- Member portal with profile management
- Access control integration
- Custom mobile app or PWA
- Advanced SEO strategy
- AI chatbot for lead capture

## What Determines the Final Price?

### 1. Number of Pages

A 3-page site costs less than a 15-page site with class schedules, trainer profiles, blog, and member portal.

### 2. Custom Features

- Online booking system
- Payment gateway (Stripe, Razorpay)
- Integration with gym management software
- Member portal

### 3. Design Complexity

Custom illustrations, animations (Framer Motion), and branded design cost more than template-based designs — but the result is completely unique to your brand.

### 4. Content Creation

Copywriting, professional photography, and video production add to the cost. Some agencies include basic copy; others charge separately.

### 5. Ongoing Costs

- Domain name: $10–$15/year
- Hosting: $20–$50/month
- SSL certificate: Free (included)
- Maintenance & updates: $50–$200/month
- SEO & marketing: $300–$1,000/month
